🌍 World War II

Overview

World War II lasted from 1939 to 1945 and involved the vast majority of the world’s nations. It was fought between the Allies, including the United States, Soviet Union, and UK, and the Axis powers, including Germany, Italy, and Japan.

Causes

WW2 was caused by unresolved issues from WW1, rise of fascist regimes, territorial expansion by Germany and Japan, and the failure of the League of Nations to maintain peace.

Major Events

Significant events included the German invasion of Poland, the Battle of Britain, Pearl Harbor attack, D-Day invasion, and the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

End of the War

WW2 ended with the unconditional surrender of Germany in May 1945 and Japan in September 1945. The United Nations was formed to prevent future conflicts.

Impact

The war caused over 70 million deaths, massive destruction, and led to the Cold War between the US and Soviet Union. It changed the political and social landscape globally.
