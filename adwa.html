⚔️ Battle of Adwa

Overview

The Battle of Adwa occurred on March 1, 1896, between Ethiopian forces led by Emperor Menelik II and Italian invaders. Ethiopia achieved a decisive victory, preserving its independence during the colonial era.

Causes

Italy attempted to colonize Ethiopia as part of the “Scramble for Africa”. Disputes over the Treaty of Wuchale escalated tensions leading to full-scale war.

Battle Details

Ethiopian forces, using superior knowledge of terrain and well-coordinated troops, outmaneuvered the Italians. The victory was significant for African resistance against European colonization.

Impact

The victory at Adwa inspired anti-colonial movements across Africa, secured Ethiopian sovereignty, and established Menelik II as a legendary military leader. Italy suffered a humiliating defeat.
