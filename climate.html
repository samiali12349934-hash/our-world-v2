What is Climate?

Climate refers to the long-term pattern of weather conditions in a region over many years. It includes temperature, rainfall, humidity, wind, and seasonal patterns. Climate is different from weather. Weather changes daily, while climate describes trends over decades.

Major Climate Zones

  • Tropical – Hot and humid year-round.
  • Dry – Very little rainfall.
  • Temperate – Moderate temperatures.
  • Continental – Cold winters, warm summers.
  • Polar – Extremely cold all year.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. Today, climate change is mainly caused by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial pollution.

Main Causes

  • Burning coal, oil, and gas
  • Deforestation
  • Industrial emissions
  • Transportation pollution
  • Agriculture methane emissions

Effects of Climate Change

  • Rising global temperatures
  • Melting ice caps
  • Sea level rise
  • Extreme weather events
  • Loss of biodiversity

Solutions

Switching to renewable energy, planting trees, reducing waste, and using sustainable transportation are powerful steps to fight climate change.
