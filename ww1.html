🌍 World War I

Overview

World War I, also called the Great War, took place from 1914 to 1918. It involved most of the world’s major powers divided into two alliances: the Allies and the Central Powers. The war began after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria.

Causes of WW1

Main causes included militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism. European powers competed for colonies and military dominance, leading to tensions that erupted into war.

Major Battles

The war saw battles like the Battle of the Somme, Verdun, and Ypres. Trench warfare and new military technologies caused unprecedented casualties and devastation across Europe.

End of the War

The war ended with the signing of the Treaty of Versailles in 1919. Millions had died, and the map of Europe was redrawn, sowing seeds for future conflicts, including World War II.

Impact

World War I led to major social, political, and economic changes. Empires collapsed, and countries faced financial hardship. It also inspired new international organizations to prevent future wars.
