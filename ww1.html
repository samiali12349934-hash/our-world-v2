World War I (1914–1918)

Main Causes

The war was caused by Militarism, Alliances, Imperialism, and Nationalism (MAIN). The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand triggered the conflict.

Alliances

The Allied Powers included Britain, France, Russia, and later the USA. The Central Powers included Germany, Austria-Hungary, and the Ottoman Empire.

Trench Warfare

Soldiers fought in trenches for protection. Conditions were harsh, with mud, disease, and constant attacks.

End of the War

The war ended in 1918. The Treaty of Versailles punished Germany heavily.

Impact

Over 16 million people died. The war reshaped Europe and led to World War II.
