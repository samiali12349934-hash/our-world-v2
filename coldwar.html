🕊 Cold War

Overview

The Cold War (1947–1991) was a period of political and military tension between the United States and the Soviet Union. It was marked by ideological conflict, nuclear arms race, and proxy wars.

Causes

The Cold War stemmed from distrust between the US and USSR, competition for global influence, and differing economic and political systems: capitalism vs communism.

Major Events

Key events included the Berlin Blockade, Korean War, Cuban Missile Crisis, Vietnam War, Space Race, and NATO/Warsaw Pact formation.

Impact

The Cold War led to a global arms race, political tensions in Europe, Africa, Asia, and Latin America, and shaped international relations for decades. It ended with the collapse of the USSR in 1991.
